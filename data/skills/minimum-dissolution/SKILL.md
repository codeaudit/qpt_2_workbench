# Minimum Dissolution (§11)

Dead structure is not absent structure — it persists and constrains coherence boundaries. Subtractive surgery, not reconstruction.

## Step 1 — Diagnose the mode through contact

- **Fossil** — "Was this once alive?" Yes → the whole evolved past it; trace of generative origin remains.
- **Residue** — "Is this incomplete?" Yes → the loop was running when attention withdrew.
- **Imposition** — "Was this ever recruited?" No `(○)` trace → imposed by spec, standard, or template. Most resistant: removal leaves a void with no latent structure to fill it.

Record the diagnosis on the card (`edit_card death=…`) with the evidence in its note.

## Step 2 — Strengthen the living side first

Never start with removal. Find or create the adjacent living work — the card or practice that should absorb this structure's role — and bring it toward the gate. The resolution of living structure often dissolves the dead side without direct intervention.

## Step 3 — One constraint, then wait

- Remove exactly one constraint: a single edit, a single de-scoping, a single removed obligation. Say precisely which one.
- Then stop. Sense reorganization before the next cut — with the user, not with more edits.
- **Imposition last**: living-side development must precede removal, because nothing fills the void otherwise.

## Step 4 — Choose the venue

- Small, local death: handle on the protocol board per above.
- Structural or organizational death: `promote_card` to the resolution board (Phase 0) — the resolution procedure (§29) exists for exactly this, and the card's diagnosis travels as tags.

## Hard rule

No batch dissolution. If you find yourself writing the third removal edit in a row, stop and report instead.
